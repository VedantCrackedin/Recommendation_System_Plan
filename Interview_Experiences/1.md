# Interview Experience Data Sources

## 1. Glassdoor Interview Experience

### Content Ownership
Mostly user-generated. Glassdoor interview reviews are posted anonymously by candidates, and Glassdoor moderates them using technology, human review, and user reports, so they are not purely raw user text.

### Content Type
Mostly semi-structured data. Fields like company, role, date, result, difficulty, sentiment, source, and location are structured, while interview descriptions and questions are free text. Glassdoor also has editorial interview-advice articles.

### Company / Role / Content
A typical Glassdoor interview entry includes company, role, candidate type, date, location, offer status, sentiment, difficulty, application method, interview process, and sometimes specific questions.

### Scraping Method
Glassdoor no longer supports API partnerships.

### Notes
Glassdoor interview experiences appear mainly user-submitted, not scraped from another site. Glassdoor also has Indeed job partnerships and author-written articles, but interview reviews are presented as anonymous user contributions.

### Main Content Types
- Interview reviews
- Interview questions
- Difficulty ratings
- Experience sentiment
- Application source
- Offer outcome
- Free-text process descriptions
- Role/category groupings such as software engineer, analyst, teacher, sales, data science, behavioral, technical, SQL, and Java

## 2. IGotAnOffer Interview Experience

### Content Ownership
IGotAnOffer’s interview content is mainly curated/editorial guides written by its team, often with coach or expert input. Some pages include questions or insights from real candidates, but the final content is packaged as guides, not raw user reviews.

### Content Type
Mostly semi-structured article content. Headings, sections, lists, authors, dates, categories, and metadata are structured, while the main explanatory text is unstructured. Overall, they are best classified as semi-structured articles.

### Company / Role / Content
It covers interview prep for major companies and roles like SWE, PM, TPM, data, engineering management, sales, and consulting. Content usually includes process guides, sample questions, frameworks, tips, example answers, and coaching/mock interview links.

### Scraping Method
No public API key. For compliant use, request permission, use only allowed public pages, follow Terms/robots.txt, crawl slowly if approved, and avoid private or login-only data.

### Notes
IGotAnOffer appears to create curated interview-prep guides using expert input, coach knowledge, candidate reports, and public interview patterns. Some sites may copy IGotAnOffer content, but that does not show IGotAnOffer is scraping from them.

### Main Content Types
- Interview-prep guides
- Company/role-specific process guides
- Question banks
- Behavioral, coding, system design, product, and case questions
- Sample answers
- Frameworks
- Prep plans
- Coaching/mock interview pages

## 3. GFG Interview Experience

### Content Ownership
GFG interview experiences are mainly user/community-submitted, then published in article format. GFG invites users to share interview and job experiences, so the content is mostly user-written and platform-curated, not simply copied from other sites. There is a separate page for submissions.

### Content Type
GFG interview experiences are semi-structured articles. They include titles, authors, tags, dates, headings, and round-wise sections, but the actual stories, tips, and questions are mostly free text.

### Company / Role / Content
GFG covers many companies and roles, with company-wise pages like Accenture, Adobe, Amazon, Apple, Barclays, Cisco, etc. Content usually includes role, recruitment process, coding/aptitude/technical/HR rounds, questions, prep tips, outcome, and sometimes location or experience level.

### Scraping Method
No official API key for interview-experience data.

### Notes
No clear evidence suggests GFG interview experiences are scraped. They appear to be community-submitted, published with user/author handles, and possibly edited or standardized by GFG before publishing.

### Main Content Types
- Company interview experiences
- Fresher/experienced experiences
- Exam and coding-test experiences
- Recruitment-process articles
- Company-wise question sets
- Technical/HR round descriptions
- Preparation tips

## 5. Reddit Interview Experience

### Content Ownership
Reddit interview experiences are mainly user-generated posts and comments, written in relevant subreddits and moderated by communities/Reddit rules. Best classification: user-written and community-moderated, not admin-curated or mainly copied from other sites.

### Content Type
Reddit is mostly unstructured/semi-structured. Metadata like subreddit, title, user, time, score, comments, flair, and URL is structured, while post bodies and comments are free text. Some interview posts add sections like OA, rounds, result, or tips.

### Company / Role / Content
Reddit can cover almost any company or role, from big tech and startups to consulting, finance, universities, and government jobs. Content often includes candidate stories, rounds, questions, recruiter communication, outcomes, compensation, prep advice, and user comments.

### Scraping Method
Reddit offers official access via Developer Platform, Data API, Ads API, researcher API, and embeds. For compliance, use official API routes, follow rate limits/terms, and avoid unauthorized scraping, especially at scale.

### Notes
Reddit is mainly a user-submission platform, not an interview-content scraper. Posts may include links or quotes from other sites, but interview experiences are mostly created by users and organized by communities.

### Main Content Types
- Personal interview stories
- Question dumps
- Offer/rejection threads
- Company/role-specific discussions
- Salary talks
- Resume screening
- Recruiter-process complaints
- OA/coding challenges
- Behavioral advice
- Follow-up Q&A

## 7. Medium Interview Experience

Medium interview experiences are mostly author-written blog posts. Medium lets users publish their own content, and its Terms say authors retain rights while giving Medium a license to host/distribute it. Some may be publication-edited, but they remain mainly user-generated.

### Content Type
Medium content is mainly article/blog format. Metadata like title, author, publication, date, tags, claps, responses, and URL is structured, while the article sections and interview narrative are mostly semi-structured/free text.

### Company / Role / Content
Medium can cover many companies and roles, since anyone can publish. Posts often include candidate background, application path, OA, coding/system design, behavioral/HR rounds, outcome, prep strategy, lessons learned, and resources used.

### Scraping Method
Medium has had a Publishing API, but it is mainly for publishing content to Medium, not for bulk-reading all interview articles.

### Notes
No clear evidence suggests Medium scrapes interview experiences. Medium is mainly a user-publishing platform. Authors may repost or quote/link sources, but Medium itself is not primarily an interview-experience scraper.

### Main Content Types
- Personal interview blogs
- Company-specific journeys
- Role-specific prep guides
- Coding/system-design/behavioral writeups
- Success/failure stories
- Offer/rejection reflections
- Resource lists

Medium is a broad blogging platform, not mainly an interview-review database.

### Main Content Types

### Content Type
